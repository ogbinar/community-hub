# Empathize Step — Data Engineering Pilipinas Community Hub

## Research Plan

### Objective
Understand the needs, pain points, motivations, and behaviors of each community profile before designing solutions for the Community Hub.

---

## Profile 1: Beginners / Absolute Starters

**Who they are:** Complete newcomers to data. May have heard terms like "SQL" or "Python" but never touched them. Often self-taught or just starting out.

### Key Research Questions
- What does "data engineering" mean to you in your own words?
- What was the moment that made you want to learn data skills?
- Where do you first go when you're stuck or confused?
- What's the most overwhelming thing about starting out?
- How do you know if you're making progress?

### Pain Points to Investigate
- Resource overload — too many links, not knowing where to start
- Imposter syndrome — feeling like everyone else "gets it"
- No clear feedback loop — "Am I learning the right things?"
- Fear of breaking things — afraid to experiment without guidance

### Empathy Map Prompts
| Says | Thinks/Feels | Does |
|---|---|---|
| "I don't know where to start" | Overwhelmed, unsure if they're smart enough | Scrolls Facebook group but rarely posts |
| "Is this normal?" | Anxious about asking "basic" questions | Watches YouTube tutorials passively |

---

## Profile 2: Career Switchers

**Who they are:** Professionals from non-data fields (biology, ESG, customer service, teaching) trying to break into data roles. Often under time pressure and financial constraints.

### Key Research Questions
- What was your previous role and why did you want to switch?
- What's your biggest barrier right now — skills, confidence, portfolio, network?
- How are you measuring whether you're "ready" for a data job?
- What kind of support would have helped you most during your transition?
- Are you actively applying for jobs or still in learning mode?

### Pain Points to Investigate
- No recognized credential — non-CS background feels like a liability
- Portfolio gap — don't know what projects actually impress hiring managers
- Network gap — no one in the industry to refer them
- Time poverty — juggling learning with current job/family
- Information asymmetry — don't know what skills are actually in demand

### Empathy Map Prompts
| Says | Thinks/Feels | Does |
|---|---|---|
| "I don't have a CS degree" | Imposter syndrome, comparing myself to others | Spends hours on free resources but feels stuck |
| "How do I prove I can do this?" | Anxious about job market readiness | Builds tutorials but doesn't know how to showcase them |

---

## Profile 3: Students & Recent Graduates

**Who they are:** BS Statistics, Math, CS, IT students from UP, DLSU, ADMU, TIP, etc. Still in school or recently graduated. Looking for internships, first jobs, and practical experience.

### Key Research Questions
- What skills do your professors teach vs. what companies actually ask for?
- How hard was it to find an internship? What blocked you?
- Do you feel confident building a portfolio on your own?
- What kind of mentorship would actually help you (not just generic advice)?
- Are you aware of the Philippine Skills Framework (PSF-AAI)?

### Pain Points to Investigate
- Academic curriculum is theoretical, not practical
- No real-world project experience on resume
- Internship competition is fierce and opaque
- Don't know how to stand out in applications
- Limited access to industry mentors who understand student struggles

### Empathy Map Prompts
| Says | Thinks/Feels | Does |
|---|---|---|
| "I studied this but I can't do the job" | Frustrated, disconnected from reality | Takes online courses alongside degree |
| "Everyone else has experience" | Envious of peers with internships | Applies to jobs blindly, gets ghosted |

## Profile 4: Data Analysts & BI Professionals

**Who they are:** Working professionals using Excel, SQL, Power BI, Tableau. Often in corporate roles. Comfortable with data but may feel stuck at the "analyst" level and want to grow into engineering or advanced analytics.

### Key Research Questions
- What tools do you use daily? What tools do you wish you knew?
- Have you ever wanted to move from analysis to engineering (or vice versa)?
- What's missing from your current learning resources?
- How do you stay updated on new tools and trends without spending hours searching?
- Do you feel the community is relevant to your level?

### Pain Points to Investigate
- Skill ceiling — know enough to be dangerous but not enough to advance
- Tool sprawl — too many competing platforms (Power BI vs Tableau vs Metabase)
- No clear path from analyst → engineer or analyst → data scientist
- Corporate isolation — few colleagues who share the same interests
- Fear of obsolescence — new tools every month

### Empathy Map Prompts
| Says | Thinks/Feels | Does |
|---|---|---|
| "I know Power BI but what's next?" | Uncertain about career trajectory | Watches random tutorials, jumps between topics |
| "Everyone uses different tools" | Frustrated by fragmentation | Sticks to what they know, avoids learning new stacks |

## Profile 5: Data Engineers & Architects

**Who they are:** Senior professionals building data pipelines, infrastructure, cloud platforms. Comfortable with Python, SQL, cloud (AWS/GCP/Azure), dbt, Airflow, Kafka, etc.

### Key Research Questions
- What's the hardest technical problem you've solved recently?
- How do you stay sharp on new tools and architectures?
- Do you mentor others? If so, what makes a good mentee?
- What gaps do you see in the community that even you can't fill?
- Would you contribute to open-source projects or lead workshops?

### Pain Points to Investigate
- Burnout from mentoring without structure or recognition
- Community noise — too many beginner questions when they want senior-level discussion
- No platform for sharing advanced knowledge (architecture patterns, production issues)
- Limited opportunities to collaborate on complex community projects
- Frustration with "tutorial hell" culture (beginners never building real things)

### Empathy Map Prompts
| Says | Thinks/Feels | Does |
|---|---|---|
| "I'd love to help but I'm busy" | Wants to contribute but lacks time structure | Drops advice in Facebook comments, rarely follows up |
| "We need more advanced content" | Frustrated by beginner-heavy discussions | Creates their own tutorials, barely promoted |

---

## Profile 6: Content Creators & Educators

**Who they are:** Community members who produce YouTube videos, blog posts, tutorials, or lead study groups (Josh Dev, Sandy Lauguico, Doc Ligot, etc.)

### Key Research Questions
- How do you decide what content to create?
- What's your biggest challenge in producing content?
- Do you feel recognized for your contributions?
- Would you collaborate with other creators on the Community Hub?
- What kind of support would help you reach more people?

### Pain Points to Investigate
- Visibility — great content but no platform to distribute it widely
- No collaboration infrastructure — creators don't know each other's work
- No tracking/recognition system for contributions
- Inconsistent quality across community resources
- Burnout from producing content without feedback or engagement metrics

### Empathy Map Prompts
| Says | Thinks/Feels | Does |
|---|---|---|
| "I made this video but who watched it?" | Uncertain about impact, wants recognition | Posts on social media, hopes for organic reach |
| "I wish others would collaborate" | Frustrated by working in silos | Creates solo content, rarely cross-promotes |

---

## Profile 7: Corporate / Mid-Senior Professionals

**Who they are:** CDOs, data managers, analytics leads at Philippine companies. Interested in frameworks, best practices, vendor certifications, and strategic thinking.

### Key Research Questions
- What's your role and how big is your data team?
- How do you keep your skills current while managing a team?
- Would you sponsor community members for internships or jobs?
- What kind of industry insights would be valuable to share?
- Do you see value in a professional network beyond your company?

### Pain Points to Investigate
- Isolation at senior level — few peers to bounce ideas off
- Hiring friction — can't find qualified local talent
- No structured way to give back (mentorship, speaking, reviewing)
- Limited visibility into what the community actually produces
- Desire for strategic content, not just technical tutorials

### Empathy Map Prompts
| Says | Thinks/Feels | Does |
|---|---|---|
| "I need good data talent" | Frustrated by hiring pipeline | Posts job requests in Facebook group occasionally |
| "I want to give back but how?" | Wants to contribute but doesn't know how | Rarely engages beyond occasional comments |

---

## Profile 8: Overseas Filipinos & International Members

**Who they are:** OFWs, Filipino diaspora, or international members interested in Philippine data community. The mentoring program explicitly says "Yes" to non-Philippines participation.

### Key Research Questions
- Are you based in the Philippines or overseas?
- Why did you join the community?
- How do you want to engage (learn, teach, network, contribute)?
- What barriers exist for international members?
- Would you mentor, hire, or collaborate remotely?

### Pain Points to Investigate
- Geographic mismatch — PH-focused events don't align with their timezone
- Limited opportunities to contribute meaningfully from afar
- Unclear whether they can participate in local initiatives (internships, meetups)
- Language/cultural gap — some content may be English-heavy or Taglish

### Empathy Map Prompts
| Says | Thinks/Feels | Does |
|---|---|---|
| "I'm Filipino but I'm overseas" | Wants to stay connected but unsure how | Lurks in Facebook group, rarely engages |
| "Can I help from here?" | Guilty about not being physically present | Occasionally shares resources, hopes for remote roles |

## Research Methods & Timeline

### Phase 1: Quantitative (Week 1-2)
- **Facebook Group Poll** — Broad survey (~200 responses) across all profiles
- **Discord/Community Survey Form** — Structured questionnaire with Likert scales
- **Forum Question Analysis** — Categorize last 6 months of posts by pain point

### Phase 2: Qualitative (Week 3-4)
- **1-on-1 Interviews** — 5-8 per profile (40-56 total interviews)
- **Focus Groups** — 2 focus groups (beginners + career switchers; senior professionals + creators)
- **Observation** — Monitor Facebook group and Discord for natural behavior patterns

### Phase 3: Synthesis (Week 5)
- Create **Empathy Maps** for each profile
- Identify **Common Themes** across profiles
- Draft **Problem Statements** ("How Might We..." format)
- Present findings to community leadership

---

## Deliverables

1. **Empathy Maps** — Visual maps for each of the 8 profiles
2. **User Personas** — Detailed character sketches with quotes, goals, frustrations
3. **Journey Maps** — How each profile interacts with the community over time
4. **Pain Point Matrix** — Prioritized list of problems by frequency and severity
5. **"How Might We" Statements** — Actionable problem framing for ideation
