## Background & Story

David is a Data Manager at a mid-sized tech company in Makati. He's in the ₱100k+ tier and wants to give back to the community but doesn't know how beyond occasional Facebook comments. **15.1% of Professionals are willing to mentor** — he's one of them but finds the commitment ambiguous.

He's frustrated by hiring friction: he can't find qualified local talent and wants to help build the pipeline. He values structured, time-bound mentoring (e.g., every first Thursday of the month) rather than indefinite commitments. He holds a Master's degree (7.4% of Professionals hold Master's; 18.6% at ₱100k+ vs 4.9% overall — disproportionately higher).

He's part of the 22.2% who had a side gig in the past 12 months (~1 in 5), driven more by Software Engineers (34.5%) than DA (20.9%) or DE (18.3%). He values team size and remote work as satisfaction drivers (from ML analysis alongside salary and career stage).

## Goals & Motivations

### Primary Goals
1. **Mentor with structured time commitments** — e.g., every first Thursday of the month instead of indefinite full-time commitment
2. **Connect with qualified local talent** — solve hiring friction (48.3% of employed members earn ≤₱35k)
3. **Give back through structured channels** — monthly mentoring, quarterly webinars, annual workshops

### Secondary Goals
4. **Build the data talent pipeline** — help create a sustainable flow of qualified local candidates

6. **Share strategic insights** — not just technical tutorials but frameworks, best practices, and hiring guidance

## Pain Points (Quantified)

### 1. Hiring Friction
> "I need good data talent, but I can't find qualified local candidates. Everyone earns ₱35k or below."

- **Evidence:** 48.3% of employed members earn ≤₱35k/month; hiring friction is a real pain point for corporate professionals
- **Impact:** Can't find qualified local talent; wants to help build the pipeline but doesn't know how

### 2. Mentorship Commitment Ambiguity
> "I'd love to help the community, but I'm busy with my own team. If there was a structured way to mentor — maybe just 2 hours a month — I'd sign up tomorrow."

- **Evidence:** 15.1% of Professionals are willing to mentor — but commitment is ambiguous; needs structured, time-bound mentoring
- **Impact:** Wants to give back but doesn't know how to structure it without overwhelming schedule

### 3. No Structured Way to Give Back
> "I post job requests in Facebook group occasionally, but that's about it. I want to do more."

- **Evidence:** No structured way to give back beyond occasional Facebook comments; 38.8% willing to share knowledge, 25.9% willing to co-train
- **Impact:** Frustrated by lack of structured channels for senior professionals to contribute meaningfully

### 4. Satisfaction is Salary-Driven, Not Role-Driven
> "I'm happy at ₱150k+, but I see others stuck at ₱35k with only 17.7% satisfaction."

- **Evidence:** High earners (₱100k+) show satisfaction scores of 8-10 at 70.9%; those at ≤₱35k at 17.7%. Career Shifters exhibit lowest satisfaction (69.0% at 1-5 scores)
- **Impact:** Wants to help narrow the income mobility gap but needs structured pathways
