## Background & Story

Maya is a 3rd-year Statistics student at a Manila university who joined the Data Engineering Pilipinas Facebook group after hearing about DataCamp scholarships from a classmate. She's time-rich and highly motivated: **81.6% of Students want upskilling**. She watches YouTube tutorials (65.8% of Students use YouTube) on weekends while balancing coursework.

She's the most AI-native segment in the community — she uses ChatGPT (86.6%), Gemini (68.2%), and GitHub Copilot (24.7%) daily for study, not as a cheat but as her primary learning medium. She doesn't see AI as a productivity hack; she sees it as "the medium through which she learns."

She wants to land an internship or entry-level data role after graduation but feels overwhelmed by the sheer volume of free resources on dataengineering.ph and other platforms. She doesn't know where to start between SQL, Python, Power BI, or cloud platforms.

## Pain Points (Quantified)

### 1. Resource Overload
> "Too many links on dataengineering.ph; I don't know where to start."

- **Evidence:** Students learn through YouTube (65.8%), DataCamp (49.3%), GitHub (37.1%) — a mix of video how-to's and hands-on projects, but no clear path
- **Impact:** Paralysis by analysis; spends hours browsing resources without building anything

### 2. DAX Knowledge Gap
> "I don't even know what DAX is."

- **Evidence:** Only 2.2% of Students use DAX vs 20.6% of Professionals — a meaningful differentiator for Data Analyst roles
- **Impact:** Missing a high-value upskill opportunity that separates capable Power BI users from experts (29.1% of Pros use it)

### 3. Hardware Bottleneck
> "My laptop can't run Docker or Spark."

- **Evidence:** 84.5% rely on laptop as primary device; only 0.8% have workstation access
- **Impact:** Can't complete projects that require cloud resources or powerful machines (e.g., DataTalks.Club DE Zoomcamp used by only 1.0% — high quality but requires Docker/Spark)

### 4. AI Dependency Risk
> "I use AI to get answers, not to learn."

- **Evidence:** 65.8% of Students use AI daily for study — significantly higher than Professionals (54.9%) or Career Shifters (53.1%)
- **Impact:** Over-indexes on GitHub Copilot (24.7% vs 13.6% Seeking work) and Blackbox (8.7%) — uses it for practical coding assistance but hasn't learned how to validate AI outputs
