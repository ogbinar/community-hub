## Background & Story

Rico is a mid-level Data Analyst working in a corporate BPO in Makati. He's been in his current role for 2 years and wants to break through to the ₱100k+ tier. He uses Power BI (69.1% of DAs specifically) daily but feels stuck at the mid-level salary band.

He knows AI is important — **65.3% use AI daily for work** (highest of any segment) — but he's not sure how to differentiate himself beyond "I use ChatGPT." He's in the modal salary band (₱35k-₱75k) with only 31.9% satisfaction scores, and he wants more.

He conducts quiet market checks at the 18-month mark (median tenure at current salary is 1-2 year band). If no raise in 2+ years, he begins active job market exploration. He's heard that ₱100k+ earners use multiple tools (Tableau, Looker Studio, Python), but doesn't know how to transition from "one tool master" to "multi-tool fluent."

## Pain Points (Quantified)

### 1. The ₱75k-₱100k Bottleneck
> "I've been at ₱55k for 2 years. I see people earning ₱100k+ — what do they know that I don't?"

- **Evidence:** Only 9.1% of Professionals sit in the ₱75k-₱100k band — a structural ceiling that requires deliberate upskilling to break through
- **Impact:** Stuck at mid-level salary; satisfaction is only 31.9% (vs 70.9% at ₱100k+)

### 2. "One BI Tool" Advice is Only Half-Right
> "I know Power BI inside out, but I see ₱100k+ earners using Tableau and Looker Studio too."

- **Evidence:** The "one BI tool" advice is correct for entry-level positioning. But ₱100k+ earners show multi-tool fluency: Tableau (38.0%), Google Looker Studio (17.7%), Python custom tools (16.5%), Alteryx (10.1%), Qlik Sense (8.9%)
- **Impact:** Power BI adoption is high across all bands — it is not a differentiator in itself; needs to add secondary tools

### 3. AI is Mainstream, Not Differentiating
> "Everyone says they use AI. How do I stand out?"

- **Evidence:** 65.3% use AI daily for work — highest of any segment. AI proficiency is no longer a differentiator among Professionals; the edge now lies in *how* you use AI (audit outputs, security, accuracy, cost)
- **Impact:** Needs to articulate specific AI workflow ("I use Copilot for query generation, Claude for documentation review") rather than just "yes, I use AI"

### 4. Tool Sophistication Gap at ₱100k+
> "₱100k+ earners use more SQL (76.9%), Python (73.6%), and niche tools like Tableau (38.0%), Alteryx (10.1%)."

- **Evidence:** Higher earners use significantly more niche or advanced BI tooling; Power BI adoption is high across all bands — it is not a differentiator in itself
- **Impact:** Needs to develop multi-tool fluency to break through the salary bottleneck
